Arithmetic Flashcards

Q1: What are the natural numbers (ℕ)?
The numbers used for counting: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, …
Q2: What are negative numbers?
Numbers less than zero, written with a minus sign (−1, −2, −3, …).
Q3: What are integers (ℤ)?
All natural and negative numbers together, including zero.
Q4: What are rational numbers (ℚ)?
Numbers that can be written as a fraction a/b, where a and b are integers and b ≠ 0.
Q5: What are irrational numbers?
Numbers that cannot be written as a fraction, like √2 or π.
Q6: What are real numbers (ℝ)?
All rational and irrational numbers together.
Q7: What is the correct order of operations?
Parentheses → Powers/Roots → Multiplication/Division → Addition/Subtraction.
Q8: Result of 5 + 7 ⋅ 2?
5 + 14 = 19 (follow order of operations).
Q9: Opposite of +3 on the number line?
−3 (opposite numbers, same distance from zero).
Q10: What happens when you add a negative number?
You move left on the number line (same as subtraction).
Q11: Rule for subtracting a negative number?
a − (−b) = a + b
